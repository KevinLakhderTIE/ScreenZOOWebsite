Daily Facts
Tyrannosaurus Rex

Tyrannosaurus rex, often simply called T. rex, is arguably the most famous dinosaur of all time. This massive predator roamed the Earth during the Late Cretaceous period, about 68 to 66 million years ago.

Name Origin:

The name "Tyrannosaurus rex" means "Tyrant Lizard King" in Greek. It was named by paleontologist Henry Fairfield Osborn in 1905.

Bite Force:

T. rex had one of the most powerful bites of any land animal in history. Its bite force is estimated to have been between 8,000 and 12,000 pounds-force (35,000 to 53,000 newtons).

Vision:

T. rex had excellent binocular vision, which means its eyes were forward-facing, allowing it to judge distances accurately. This was likely an adaptation for hunting.

Feathers:

While early depictions of T. rex showed it with scaly skin, recent discoveries and research suggest that it may have had feathers, especially when it was young. These feathers would have been more like fuzz rather than the feathers of modern birds.

T. rex remains one of the most studied and celebrated dinosaurs in paleontology, and ongoing research continues to shed light on its behavior, physiology, and the world it inhabited.
